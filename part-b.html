Sparse but Connected

Density of ~0.007 means only 0.7% of all possible actor pairs are connected — typical for large collaboration networks where most actors work in different productions.

The right-skewed distribution is consistent with qualitative heavy-tail behavior. A small number of prolific actors (hubs) have dramatically more connections than the median, though a formal power-law fit was not computed.

Assortativity

Degree assortativity measures whether high-degree actors tend to connect with other high-degree actors. In Hollywood, prolific actors often appear together in ensemble films.

03 — Centrality

Actor Centrality Analysis

Five centrality metrics are computed on the GCC to capture different dimensions of actor importance in the collaboration network. Together they identify both hubs and bridge actors.

Degree

Number of Connections

Raw count of distinct co-stars. Captures prolific actors who appear in many films alongside many different people.

Strength

Weighted Degree

Sum of edge weights. Identifies actors with strong recurring partnerships — repeat collaborators in franchise films.

Closeness

Network Reachability

How quickly an actor can "reach" any other actor through short paths. High closeness = well-positioned in the network.

Betweenness

Bridge Actor Score

Fraction of shortest paths that pass through an actor. High betweenness = bridge actor connecting otherwise separate communities.

Eigenvector

Influence Score

Centrality based on the importance of neighbors. An actor connected to other highly central actors scores highest.

06 — Discussion

Findings, Limitations & Next Steps

What Worked

  • · The weighted actor network is well-connected enough to support robust centrality and community analysis.
  • · Louvain detection produced interpretable communities with a meaningful modularity signal above the random-graph baseline.
  • · Community-level TF-IDF reveals vocabulary differences that support the genre specialization hypothesis.

Known Limitations

  • · Popularity bias: TMDB popularity sampling over-represents mainstream blockbusters — niche or art-house film communities may be undersampled.
  • · Cast rank cutoff: Limiting to top-8 actors per film excludes supporting and character actors who may form distinct communities.
  • · Text duplication: Movies with actors in multiple communities contribute their overview text to all linked communities, diluting distinctiveness.
  • · No formal power-law test: Heavy-tail interpretation is qualitative; a rigorous goodness-of-fit test was not performed.

Potential Next Steps

  • → Add temporal slices to test whether community structure and bridge actors are stable over 2010–2025.
  • → Compare Louvain with an alternative algorithm (e.g., Girvan-Newman) and measure partition agreement.
  • → Apply lemmatization and phrase detection to improve text distinctiveness in community language profiles.
  • → Extend cast rank cutoff and test sensitivity of community structure to different k values.
